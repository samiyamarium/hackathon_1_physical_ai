# Bibliography

This bibliography serves as an extended resource list, complementing the formal references provided in the "References" section. While the references focus on directly cited works that informed the core arguments and technical details within this book, the bibliography aims to offer a broader spectrum of influential texts, foundational research, and comprehensive overviews that have shaped the understanding of robotics, artificial intelligence, and sophisticated software development methodologies.

The selections here encompass a diverse range of topics, from the theoretical underpinnings of AI and autonomous systems to practical guides on software architecture and project management in complex, multi-agent environments. It includes seminal works that have defined their respective fields, as well as contemporary publications that reflect the current state-of-the-art in AI-robot integration and AI-assisted development. This compilation is intended to guide readers who wish to delve deeper into the subjects presented, providing avenues for further exploration and enhancing a holistic comprehension of the innovative approaches adopted by the Humanoid Robot project.

We encourage readers to utilize this bibliography not just as a list of sources, but as a roadmap for continuous learning and intellectual engagement with the rapidly evolving domains of robotics and artificial intelligence. The interdisciplinary nature of this project necessitates a broad base of knowledge, and this bibliography reflects that imperative, offering a rich tapestry of thought from leading experts and institutions worldwide.
